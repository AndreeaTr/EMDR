Frequently Asked Questions
What is EMDR therapy?

EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing) is a psychotherapy technique that was developed to help people cope with the pain of painful memories (Shapiro, 1989a, 1989b). According to Shapiro's (2001) Adaptive Information Processing model, EMDR counseling helps people access and interpret painful memories and other negative life experiences in order to reach an adaptive resolution.
